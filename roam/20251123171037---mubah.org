#+title: Mubah

* linguistically
:PROPERTIES:
:ID:       3fe489fe-4865-42b1-93fe-bfc7ecb2469d
:END:
means something declared or permitted.
* Technically
:PROPERTIES:
:ID:       d9416941-f758-464b-8d72-0045797be5ef
:END:
It is also refereed to as halal and ja'iz.
it refers to
** what is neither connected with a command nor prohibited in and of itself,
like eating during the night of Ramadan.



** excludes

:ID:       56143647-3c37-420f-97e4-f9753fa641fc
:END:
*** what is connected with a command
:PROPERTIES:
:ID:       37f8731b-2fd9-4b02-94e0-45badfd72790

- wajib (obligatory)
- mandud (recommended).
*** not something prohibited

-  muharram (forbidden)
-  makruh(disliked).

*** in and out itself

:ID:       bef6ce76-2df2-4459-8f70-e7aef6162887
:END:
**** excludes anything that acts as a medium leading to a commanded actions
:PROPERTIES:

- thus becoming obligatory, or leading to a forbidden actions,
- thus becoming prohibited, which would alter its original status as mubah (permissible).
- When something is categorised as mubah (permissible), it remains within the bounds of permissibility, not resulting in reward or punishment for engaging in it.
