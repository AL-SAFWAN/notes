* General & Specific

* General Texts / Terms
** Definition
"An all-encompassing word that includes all its individual elements without restriction."

Or:
"A word that encompasses everything it applies to, according to a single usage, without limitation."

** Ruling
- It is obligatory to act upon the general wording.
- No requirement to search for specifying evidence unless present.

** Expressions that indicate Generality
1. Explicit general terms:
   - كل، جميع، كافة، قاطبة
   - “all, every, whole, entire”
2. أسماء الشرط — Nouns of condition
3. أسماء الاستفهام — Interrogative pronouns
4. الأسماء الموصولة — Relative pronouns
5. نكرة في سياق النفي، الشرط، أو النهي — Indefinite nouns in:
   - negation
   - condition
   - prohibition
6. المعرف بالإضافة — Definite noun by possession
7. المعرف بالألف واللام الاستغراقية — Generic noun with ال of total inclusion

** Categories of General Terms
1. العام الباقي على عمومه — Remains general  
2. العام المخصوص — General that is specified  
3. العام المراد به الخصوص — General but intended specific  
4. العام الوارد على سبب خاص — General wording revealed for a specific reason

* Specific Texts / Terms
** Definition
"A word that specifies a general term to restrict it to a person or a certain number."

** Ruling
- Scholars agree: specific terms definitively indicate their meaning.
- The legal ruling of a specific referent must be acted upon.

** How Specific Terms Work
1. Allah commands a specific act of worship.
2. Allah prohibits a specific act.
3. Allah specifies a number of times an act is done.
4. Allah lifts the rule of a general term for:
   - a group
   - a person
   - a number of occurrences

** Examples
1. Commanding a specific act
   - {أقيموا الصلاة وآتوا الزكاة واركعوا مع الراكعين} (2:43)

2. Prohibiting a specific act
   - {ولا تقتلوا النفس التي حرم الله إلا بالحق} (17:33)

3. Specifying number
   - Feeding *ten* needy (5:89)
   - Fasting *three* days (2:196)
   - Zakat: “In every forty sheep, one sheep.”

* At-Takhsis — Specifying a General Text
** Definition
"Singling out specific elements from a general term."

** Two Main Types
1. *Connected* (المتصل – al-Mukhassas al-Muttasil)
2. *Separate* (المنفصل – al-Mukhassas al-Munfasil)

* al-Mukhassas al-Muttasil — Connected Specifier
Occurs *within the same sentence or context.*

Includes:
1. Istithna’ (exception)
2. Sifah (description/attribute)
3. Shart (condition)

** 1. Istithna’ – Exception
Example (Qur’an):
- {من كفر بالله من بعد إيمانه إلا من أكره...}

Example (Sunnah):
- “Its trees shall not be cut *except* idhkhir grass.”

** 2. Sifah – Descriptive Attribute
Example:
- Marriage to believing slave women (4:25)
- Zakat on “freely grazing” sheep

** 3. Shart – Condition
Example:
- Husband inherits half *if* she has no children (4:12)

* al-Mukhassas al-Munfasil — Separate Specifier
The restricting evidence comes from *another text*, not the same sentence.

Sources:
- Qur’an by Qur’an
- Qur’an by Sunnah
- Qur’an by Ijmaa’
- Qur’an by Qiyas
- Sunnah by Qur’an
- Sunnah by Sunnah
- Sunnah by Ijmaa’
- Sunnah by Qiyas
