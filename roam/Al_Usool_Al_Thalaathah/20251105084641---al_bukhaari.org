#+title: Al-Bukhaari
#+filetags: :Imaam:

* Bio
*Name*: Imaan Abū ‘Abd Allāh Muḥammad ibn Ismā‘īl ibn Ibrāhīm ibn al-Mughīrah ibn Bardizbah al-Ju‘fī al-Bukhārī
*Born*: 194AH
*Died*: 256AH
*Age*: 62 
*Titles*: Amīr al‑Muʾminīn fī al‑Ḥadīth (Leader of the Faithful in ḥadīth)

* Teachers
- *Yaḥyā ibn Ma'īn* (d. 233 AH)
   The pre-eminent critic of narrators (rijāl). Al-Nasā'ī classed him with the top four of that age. 
- *Alī ibn al-Madīnī* (d. 234 AH)
   Bukhārī said: “I never felt small before anyone except 'Alī ibn al-Madīnī.” A master of 'ilal (hidden defects).
   His teacher said, Bukhari has never seen anyone like him self
- *Isḥāq ibn Rāhwayh* (d. 238 AH)
   senior imām; his “why don’t you compile a book of only ṣaḥīḥ ḥadīth?” remark sparked Bukhārī’s project. 
- *Ahmad ibn hanbal* (d. 241 AH)
   Bukhārī met and learned from him; there’s very little direct narration from Aḥmad in the Ṣaḥīḥ (Ibn Ḥajar notes one direct spot and one via intermediary). 

* Students
- Muḥammad b. Yūsuf al-Fīrabrī (d. 320 AH)
   main transmitter; most printed editions descend from his recension. He said, “~70,000 people heard Ṣaḥīḥ with me.” 
- Imām Muslim ibn al-Ḥajjāj (d. 261 AH)
   attended and narrated from al-Bukhārī. One of the foremost students; author of Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim. 
- Imām Abū ʿĪsā al-Tirmidhī (d. 279 AH)
   explicitly described as a pupil of al-Bukhārī in standard bios; author of Jāmiʿ al-Tirmidhī. 
- Imām al-Nasāʾī (d. 303 AH)
   counted among those who narrated from al-Bukhārī; author of Sunan al-Nasāʾī. 
- Muḥammad b. Naṣr al-Marwazī (d. 294 AH)
   also listed among those who narrated from al-Bukhārī (major early imām). 

* His father was a pious man
His father a student of Imaan malik, Ismael, was known for piety and scrupulous earnings. Reports mention he said he did not know of any unlawful coin in his wealth. He died when al‑Bukhārī was young.

* He stood out in memory and understanding
Memorised Qur'an at 7. He memorised narrations, he went of the lesson of the senior teacher ad-Dakhili, he was narrating hadith of abu al-zubayr, at the time and raised his hand and said you did not narrate from Ibrahim al-Nakha'i. Imaan Bukhaari said go back to your book and check, he went back to his book and he made the mistake and ask him to correct him every time he made a mistake. 

* Al-Tarikh Al-Kabir 
He memories everything about the narrator and compiles the book at the age of 18. He went for Ummrah and stay in Medina, he sat at the rawdah and he would sit the in the full night to write tariq al kabir, it printed in 21 volumes he write all the biography of the narrators with details, (birth/death, reliability and kunays).
- There was many stories in so he remove it to some, out of fear it will become too large.
- A Scholar took this kitab to the leader of bukhara and said should I show you magic, and he showed this kitab. He written the biography of 40,000 narrators before he was even know. 
- They said his memory was photographic


* Narrating to others in different cities, using the knowledge of the isnad 
He was someone know by the uleema and student of knowleadge of his time. For example, he would go to basarah and say I will narrate to you a hadith that you have never heard (because he knew that they were from basarah and they haven't heard certain hadith, as it did not reach them.) He will follow the same pattern in other cities, because he know who's in the chain of these narrations, an knows that no one from basarah has me them (the new city) and he will narrate to them to spread the knowleadge that didn't come.

* Tested by other muhaditheen 
- In Samarqand, he was test be 400 muhadith of for seven days, they will mention narration, mixing isnad, and they couldn't find a single mistake.
- In Baghdad, He was tested by 10 each narrating 10 hadith. They change the chain of the narration, and Imaan bukhaari would say I don't know to each of the altered version, then he recited all 100 back in their mistake that they made and then provided them with the correct version.

* His Ibadah
- Finished the Qur'an 40 times in Ramadan and 1 every 3 nights outside of Ramadan 
- He would spend most his wealth on hadith, to the point where he had no wealth, and would only grass. Someone would come with a bag of dirhams and just walk away, this was a dua that was accepted. 
- Before writing a narration he would make dua and preform 2 rakat so that Allah can guild him to that which was correct, he went thought 600,000 chain, just to compile sahih al bukhaari
