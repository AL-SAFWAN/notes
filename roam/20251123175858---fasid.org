#+title: Fasid

linguistically
something that has been corrupted or is at a loss.
Technically
action or contract that fail to produce their intended legal effect.
- are those that do not fulfil the obligation required, resulting in an unmet religious demand


All corrupt acts of worship, contracts and related condition are considered [[id:3e653a3f-bd40-4551-b7fc-28c32b7ec903][haram]] (forbidden); this is viewed as overstepping Allah's limits and mocking His verse, especially since the prophet Muhammad (ï·º) forbade stipulating conditions that are not sanctioned by the Book of Allah.

Example
Fasid (invalid) action of worship
- such as praying before the appointed time.
  
Fasid (invalid) contracts
- to sell something unspecified or unknown.


While fasid (invalid) and batil (false) are generally synonymous, they differ distinctly in two specific scenario:

In Ihram:

        Fasid applies to violations by a person in Ihram, such as wearing stitched clothing or covering the head for males before the first restriction of Ihram are lifted.
        
        On the other hand batil (false) described more severs infractions, such as renouncing Islam while in Ihram

In Marriage:
        Fasid refers to conditions within a marriage that scholars have debated over, such as the validity of a marriage without a valid (guardian).
        batil, however, refers to conditions unanimously recognised as invalid, such as the marriage of a widow or a divorced woman during her waiting period, which is universally accepted as invalid.

(These distinctions highlight the specific legal consequence and religious implications associated with each term, reflecting the depth and complexity of Islamic jurisprudential terminology)
