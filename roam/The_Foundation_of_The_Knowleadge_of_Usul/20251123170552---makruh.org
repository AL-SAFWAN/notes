#+title: Makruh
* Synonyms
** 1. Historical Usage
- Early scholars used makrūh to mean:
- ḥarām, o disliked
- Many, like Imām Aḥmad, used makrūh to mean ḥarām out of caution.
  - How to know what they meant?
    - Look at:
            Their students’ explanations
            Context of the ruling
Today: makrūh = disliked, not sinful.


** 2. Makrūh in the Ḥanafī Madhhab
*** Makrūh Taḥrīmī = like haram
Evidence: qaṭʿī meaning, ẓannī proof.
Ruling: Close to ḥarām, sinful to do, *leaving is obligatory*.
*** Makrūh Tanzīhī - like makruh
Evidence: ẓannī dislike.
Ruling: Disliked, not sinful; *leaving is rewarded*.

* Points of Imaan 
•Does not necessitate a punishment in this world or the next
•Constantly doing makrooh will eventually lead to one falling into haram
