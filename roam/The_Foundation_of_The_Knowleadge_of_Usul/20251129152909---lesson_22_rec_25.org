* The categories of evidences (al-Adillah الأدلّة)
** Evidences agreed upon (أدلة متفق عليها)
1. The Qur’an (القرآن)
2. The Sunnah (السنة)
3. Consensus – /ijmāʿ/ (الإجماع)
4. Analogy – /qiyās/ (القياس)

** Disputed evidences (أدلة مختلف عليها)
1. Statements of the Companions (قول الصحابي)
2. al-Istihsān (الاستحسان)
3. al-Maṣlaḥah al-Mursalah (المصلحة المرسلة)
4. The law of previous nations – /sharʿ man qablana/ (شرع من قبلنا)

** Statement of Imām al-Shāfiʿī
قال الشافعي: «وجهة العلم: في الكتاب أو السنَّة أو الإجماع أو القياس»
- “The foundation of knowledge is: a report from the Book (the Qur’an),
  or the Sunnah, or consensus (ijmāʿ), or analogy (qiyās).”

* Usul al-Fiqh – recap
** Definition
- “A general knowledge of:
  - the *evidences* of fiqh,
  - the *manner* of deriving rulings from them,
     - some of them put this at the end of the chapter of the one who takes the ruling
       - because they are the one who exersice this 
  - and the *standing* of the one who is able to derive rulings.”

** Three pillars
1. *Evidences of fiqh* (الأدلة)
2. *The manner of deriving rulings* – /dalālāt al-alfāẓ/ (دلالات الألفاظ)
3. *The one able to derive rulings* – /al-mujtahid/ (المجتهد)
