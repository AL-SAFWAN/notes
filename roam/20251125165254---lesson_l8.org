* Commands and prohibitions

** Commands (الأمر)
*** Definition
A command (amr) is:

- “A statement that includes the request of an action by way of a high authority.”

*** Forms of commandments
- The imperative verb
- The imperative verbal noun
- The verbal noun used in place of the imperative verb
- Present tense verb joined with the *lām* of imperative
- Other forms

*** What does a command necessitate?
1. The default position of all commands is that they necessitate obligation (*wujūb*).
2. Actions must be carried out at the earliest time.
3. Fulfilling the commandment once.
4. The prohibition of the opposite.

*** Other meanings of commandments
With the presence of an external indicator (*qarīnah*), or via the
context of a verse, a command could also mean:

- Something recommended
- Something permissible
- A threat

** Important principles for commandments in uṣūl al-fiqh

*** Principle 1
األمر يدل على الوجوب ::
The command indicates obligation.

*** Principle 2
األمر يقتضي الفور ::
The command necessitates immediacy.

*** Principle 3
األمر المطلق ال يقتضي التكرار ::
An unrestricted command does not necessitate repetition.

*** Principle 4
األمر بالشيء نهي عن ضده ::
Commanding something is a prohibition of its opposite.

*** Principle 5
األمر بعد الحظر يفيد ما كان عليه ذلك الشيء قبل ورود األمر ::
A command issued after a prohibition signifies what the matter was
upon before the command was given.

*** Principle 6
الخبر بمعنى األمر يدل على الوجوب ::
An informative report in the sense of a command signifies obligation.

*** Principle 7
األمر يفيد الوجوب بال قرينة، وبالقرينة يفيد ما تفيد القرينة ::
A command signifies obligation without a context, and with context it
signifies what the context indicates.

*** Principle 8
الوسائل لها أحكام المقاصد ::
Means take on the rulings of their objectives.

*** Principle 9
ما ال يتم الواجب إال به فهو واجب ::
Whatever is necessary to complete an obligation is itself obligatory.

* Prohibitions (النهي)

** Definition
A prohibition (nahy) is:

- “A statement which includes a request to avoid doing something by
  way of a high authority.”

** Forms of prohibitions
- The *lām* of prohibition
- Other forms

** What does a prohibition necessitate?
1. The default position of all prohibitions is that they necessitate
   *taḥrīm* (forbidden).
2. Invalidity of the action if the prohibition returns to “the very
   thing itself being prohibited.”
3. Actions must be avoided right away.
4. Continuous avoidance of the action.
5. The command of the opposite.

** Other meanings of prohibitions
With the presence of an external indicator, or via the context of a
verse, a prohibition may also mean:

- Dislike
- Guidance

** Does a prohibition necessitate the invalidity of the action?

النهي :: Prohibition

- ان يكون النهي عائدا إلى أمر خارج ال يتعلق بذات المنهي عنه وال شرطه ::
  The prohibition returns back to something other than the specific
  prohibited action or its conditions.

- ان يكون النهي عائدا إلى شرط المنهي عنه ::
  The prohibition returns back to one of the conditions of the
  prohibited action.

- ان يكون النهي عائدا ذات المنهي عنه ::
  The prohibition returns back to the very action itself being
  prohibited.

** Important principles for prohibitions in uṣūl al-fiqh

*** Principle 1
النهي يدل على التحريم ::
Prohibitions indicate impermissibility.

*** Principle 2
األصل في النهي التحريم حتى تأتي قرينة تصرفه إلى الكراهة ::
The default rule regarding prohibitions is impermissibility unless
there is evidence that redirects it to dislike.
- the evidence is 6 types the above we just did
*** Principle 3
النهي المطلق للفور والدوام ::
An absolute prohibition implies immediate and continuous avoidance.

*** Principle 4
النهي يدل على الفساد ::
A prohibition necessitates the invalidity of the action.
*** Principle 5
ما ال يتم الحرام اال به فهو حرام ::
Whatever is necessary to complete a prohibition is itself prohibited.

* Exercises

** Exercise 1 – Sūrah al-Ḥujurāt (49:10–12)
- Verses: Qur’an 49:10–12.
- Themes:
  - Brotherhood of the believers.
  - Prohibition of mockery between groups and between women.
  - Prohibition of defaming and insulting with nicknames.
  - Avoiding much suspicion; some suspicion is sin.
  - Prohibition of spying and backbiting.
  - Fear of Allah and hope in His mercy.

** Exercise 2 – Sūrah an-Nisā’ (4:36)
- Verse: Qur’an 4:36.
- Themes:
  - Worship Allah alone and associate none with Him.
  - Excellence towards parents.
  - Kindness to relatives, orphans and the poor.
  - Good treatment of the near neighbour and the distant neighbour.
  - Good treatment of the companion at your side, the wayfarer, and
    those whom your right hands possess.
  - Allah does not love those who are proud and boastful.

** Exercise 3 – Sūrah al-An‘ām (6:151–153)
- Verses: Qur’an 6:151–153.
- Themes:
  - List of major prohibitions and commands:
    - Tawḥīd: do not associate partners with Allah.
    - Good treatment of parents.
    - Do not kill children out of poverty; Allah provides for you and them.
    - Do not approach shameful sins, open or secret.
    - Do not kill any soul Allah has made sacred, except with right.
    - Do not approach the orphan’s property except to improve it
      until maturity.
    - Give full measure and weight with justice.
    - Speak justly, even concerning close relatives.
    - Fulfil the covenant of Allah.
  - This is the straight path of Allah; follow it and do not follow
    other paths that separate you from His path.
