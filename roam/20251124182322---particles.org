C- The particle:
1. Particles - ‫الحروف‬:

Particles do not carry full meaning alone but serve to structure sentences and convey relationships between other words ([[id:555a5917-a056-4a45-b474-826991512081][nouns]] and [[id:2c5eb58e-075f-44e4-b0c9-7e52d26f92aa][verbs]]).
- it functions to connect, modify, or add relational meaning to the sentence
  
Examples include fi (in), min (from), and wa (and).

1. Waw: used to denote association or conjugations between two elements
   - indicating that they are linked in some form of ruling.
     e.g wudo wash you're fash and wash you're arms
               
2. Fa: severs as a connector indicating sequence and immediacy between elements
   - it often implies causality or purpose, showing why something occurs following another event
     - do directly 
       - two meaning
         1. directly after
            - if you wake up from sleep wash you're hand directly after
         2. identify a sabab/reason
            - you can use the fa to find the sabab
            - e.g why you should wash you're hand becasue you don't know where it was when you was sleeping
           
3. Genitive Lam: this particle can express several meanings:
   - reasoning (why something is done)
   - ownership (belonging to someone)
     - e.g to Allah belongs 
   - permisibility (allowing for an action)
   
4. Genitive 'Ala: Often conveys a sense of obligation,
   - indicating that the actions described is required or expected
     - fasting has been made obligatory to you
        used the "'ala " to show this
