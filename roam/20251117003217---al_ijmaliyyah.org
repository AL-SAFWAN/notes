#+title: al-ijmaliyyah
#+filetags: :terminology:

al-ijmaliyyah "general"

refers to
* broad principle or rules
:PROPERTIES:
:ID:       e6d2dd3b-3376-4b6b-9a53-8794c16ee798
:END:
- exemplified by maxims 
  - a command implies an obligation, prohibition implies forbidance
  - actions are based upon intention
    
** exclude
:PROPERTIES:
:ID:       0a9094f1-d138-407c-b1f2-1f6bc5b3e1f6
:END:
*** specific evidence 
:PROPERTIES:
:ID:       ba019a74-b637-414d-919c-9911fee8795c
:END:
  - except when used illustratively to demonstrate a general rule.

* examples
• What are the agreed upon evidences in Islam?
• How does the Sunnah constitute as an evidence?
• Do the statements of the companions constitute as an evidence?
• Can the scriptures of “the people of the book” be used as an evidence?
