#+title: He said, “Does my prayer please you?” She said, “Why wouldn’t your prayer please me?” He said, “By God, it is my prayer for my nation in every prayer.”

[[id:b79b21c1-8212-4340-a9cf-b95f3264ad89]['Aisha]] said when I saw the Messenger of Allah, may God bless him and grant him peace, in good spirits, I said, “O Messenger of God, pray for me.” He said, “O God, forgive Aisha for her past and future sins, and for what she concealed and what she made public.” Aisha laughed until her head fell into her lap. The Messenger of God, may God bless him and grant him peace, said, “Does my prayer please you?” She said, “Why wouldn’t your prayer please me?!” He said, “By God, it is my prayer for my nation in every prayer.”

Summary of the Hadith scholar's ruling: Its chain of narrators consists of men of authentic hadith except for Ahmad ibn Mansur al-Ramadi, who is trustworthy.

Narrator: Aisha, Mother of the Believers | Hadith scholar: Al-Shawkani | Source: Durr al-Sahaba, page or number: 254

 Reference: Narrated by al-Bazzar as in Kashf al-Astar by al-Haythami (2658), and Ibn Hibban (7111) with slight differences, and al-Hakim (6738) with similar wording.
