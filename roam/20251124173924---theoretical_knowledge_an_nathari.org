#+title: Theoretical Knowledge (an-nathari)

These are perceived as informed truth that require proof and extended reasoning

- e.g the necessity of intention in prayer (theoretical knowledge often demands a degree of intellectual effort to acquire and understand.)

  
theoretical knowledge is characterised as 
- requireing active learning, reflection and analysis.
- It involves concepts and information that are not immediately apparent and typically require education or personal inquiry to understand.
